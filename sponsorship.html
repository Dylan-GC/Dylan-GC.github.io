The Margaret Thatcher Statue Project

In 2013, the Grantham Community Heritage Association launched a project to raise funds to commission a statue of Margaret Thatcher. The project was a fund raising project to support the educational charity that operates the Grantham Museum where for every pound that was donated to the statue project, a certain percentage would go towards supporting the work of the Museum. We were delighted to receive cross community support for this project in recognition of the important educational work of the volunteers of the GCHA.

In 2018, we signed a cooperation agreement with The Public Memorials Appeal and South Kesteven District Council. Planning permission was unanimously approved in a public planning meeting in February 2019 for the staue to be situated on St Peters Green outside of the Grantham Museum.

We would like to thank the hundreds of people from all over the world that donated to the statue and Museum funraising project. Based on current cashlfows after our agreed contribution to the delivery of the statue - the project raised enough money to support the educational charity that operate the Grantham Museum for around 10 years.

We are proud to unveil the statue located on St Peters Green
